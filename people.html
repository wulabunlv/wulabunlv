She works in the Wu lab as a graduate assistant. Her research interests are mainly in the area of predictive model development, model selection and validation, personalized medicine, and health disparities. She is also interested in using machine learning to improve risk prediction and to enhance our understanding of the disease. Currently, she is focusing on developing statistical models with physical characteristics, clinical history, and genetic information for bone mineral density and osteoporotic fracture prediction. She is also actively involved in population-based epidemiological analyses in understanding the occurrence of disease in a population.

Outside of the lab, she is a doctoral student in Epidemiology and Biostatistics at the School of Public Health. Before coming to the UNLV, she received my Bachelor of Science in Clinical Laboratory Sciences from Dalian Medical University in 2014. In 2016, she obtained a Master of Science degree in Public Health and Biostatistics from Tulane University. In addition to research work and studies, she enjoys exercising and traveling.
